Investing doesn't have to be complicated or scary. It's simply the act of putting your money to work to build wealth over time. Here is your roadmap to getting started.

## 1. The Foundation: Clear Debt and Build an Emergency Fund

Before you invest, ensure you have:
* A plan to pay off high-interest debt (like credit cards).
* 3 to 6 months of living expenses saved in an easily accessible savings account.

## 2. Stocks vs. Funds

* **Stocks:** Buying a small ownership share in a single company (e.g., Apple). Higher risk, potentially higher reward.
* **Mutual Funds / ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds):** Buying a basket of stocks or bonds. Lower risk due to immediate diversification. **Great for beginners!**

## 3. Where to Start Investing

1.  **Retirement Accounts (Best):** Max out tax-advantaged accounts first (e.g., 401k, Roth IRA).
2.  **Brokerage Account:** Open an account with a platform like Fidelity, Schwab, or Vanguard.
3.  **Invest in Low-Cost Index Funds:** Start with a fund that tracks a major index (like the S&P 500).

## 4. The Power of Compounding

Compounding interest is your best friend. The earlier you start, the more time your money has to grow exponentially.
